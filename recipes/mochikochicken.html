Mochiko Chicken

Nori Wrapped Mochiko Chicken

Mochiko chicken is a popular Hawaii dish marinated in a mochiko flour batter. Sweet, salty and crunchy local favorite!

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds Boneless, Skinless Chicken Thighs
  • 2 cups Mochiko flour
  • 2 cups Sugar
  • 2 cups Cornstarch
  • 1 tsp Garlic Powder
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups Shoyu
  • 3 to 4 sheets Nori, cut into 1 inch strips (optional)
  • 1/4 cupGreen onions, chopped (optional)
  • Seasame Seeds (optional)
  • Neutral oil, for frying

Steps

  1. Rinse and dry the chicken.
  2. Cut chicken into 2 inch strips. Place in a bowl.
  3. In a seperate large bowl mix mochiko flour, sugar, cornstarch and garlic powder. Whisk until combined.
  4. Add in 2 eggs to dry ingredients.
  5. Mix in 1 cup of Shoyu until combined.
  6. Slowly mix in the second cup of shoyu making sure there are no clumps.
  7. Add in chopped green onions and seasame seeds if desired.
  8. Mix cut chicken into mixture.
  9. Cover and place in fridge for minimum 5 hours to overnight.
  10. Remove chicken and wrap in nori and place back into mixture 30 minutes before cooking.
  11. Fill a high-sided pot or dutch oven with oil to a depth of 2-3 inches over medium-low heat to 330-340 degrees.
  12. Add as much chicken to the pot as you can without over crowding.
  13. Fry the chicken for 6 to 7 minutes turning to brown evenly.
  14. Remove to cool for a few minutes.
  15. Continue until all the chicken has been fried golden brown.
  16. Serve with rice.
